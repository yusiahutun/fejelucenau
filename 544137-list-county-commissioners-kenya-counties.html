List of all 47 county commissioners in Kenya with their counties

Publish date: 2024-04-27

The new constitution divides Kenya into 47 counties for administrative purposes. Each county has a county commissioner whose main job is to ensure proper coordination of all national government activities at the county level. So, who are the county commissioners in Kenya?

TABLE OF CONTENTS

County commissioners in Kenya fall on the docket of The Ministry of Interior and National Administration. One of the key qualifications for a county commissioner is that you must have served in public service for at least three years as a deputy county commissioner.

What are the functions of county commissioners in Kenya?

The key function of the county commissioners in Kenya under the devolved government is to coordinate the national government functions at the county level. These functions include:

  • Coordinating security management.
  • Coordinate disaster management and emergency response.
  • Promote and enhance national cohesion and integration to foster unity of the nation.
  • Deliver quality services to the citizens of Kenya.
  • Observe good corporate governance principles in the performance of duties.
  • Coordinating national government functions and delivery of services.
  • Facilitating conflict management and peace-building.
  • Manage and maintain administrative boundaries, security roads, and airstrips.
  • Mobilising national government agencies for national events and programs.

Who appoints county commissioners in Kenya?

County commissioners in Kenya are appointed by the president or the cabinet secretary in charge of national coordination.
