The Poljud Stadium is a modern architectural marvel.

Standing proudly in the city of Split, Croatia, the Poljud Stadium is renowned for its unique design and breathtaking beauty. Its sweeping roof structure and open ends create a sense of grandeur and allow for spectacular views of the surrounding landscape. The stadium’s modern design has earned it a place on numerous lists of the world’s most impressive stadiums.

Poljud Stadium has a seating capacity of over 34,000.

With a capacity of over 34,000 spectators, Poljud Stadium is the largest stadium in the Dalmatia region of Croatia. It serves as the home ground for the popular football club, Hajduk Split. The stadium’s large capacity enables it to host both domestic and international football matches, as well as other sporting events and concerts.

The stadium was opened in 1979.

Poljud Stadium officially opened its doors on May 13, 1979, and has since become a beloved landmark in Split. Its construction was no small feat, as it required reclaiming land from the sea and overcoming various engineering challenges. Today, the stadium stands as a testament to human ingenuity and perseverance.

Poljud Stadium hosted the 1990 UEFA European Championship.

In a historic moment for Croatian football, Poljud Stadium played host to several matches during the 1990 UEFA European Championship. The stadium’s electric atmosphere and passionate fans created an unforgettable experience for players and spectators alike. This prestigious event further solidified Poljud Stadium’s reputation as a world-class sporting venue.

It is known for its unique “U” shape.

Unlike traditional stadiums, Poljud Stadium features a distinctive “U” shape. This design allows for better acoustics and optimal viewing angles, ensuring an immersive experience for all attendees. The stadium’s layout also enhances the sense of camaraderie among fans, creating an unforgettable atmosphere during matches.

Poljud Stadium offers stunning panoramic views.

Situated on the slopes of the Marjan Hill, Poljud Stadium offers breathtaking panoramic views of the Adriatic Sea and the city of Split. Whether it’s daytime or under the floodlights, spectators can enjoy the stunning backdrop while cheering on their favorite teams.

The stadium is a symbol of pride for the people of Split.

Poljud Stadium holds a special place in the hearts of the people of Split. It represents not only sporting excellence but also the spirit of unity and togetherness. The stadium has witnessed countless memorable moments and has become an integral part of the city’s identity.

Poljud Stadium has hosted international music concerts.

In addition to sporting events, Poljud Stadium has also played host to several international music concerts featuring world-renowned artists. The combination of the stadium’s unique architecture and its natural amphitheater-like setting creates a truly unforgettable experience for concert-goers.
