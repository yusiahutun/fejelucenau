How to Bring the Missing Downloads Folder Back to Your Macs Dock

Publish date: 2024-08-13

Is your Downloads folder missing from your Mac's Dock? You likely removed it by mistake. The Downloads folder sits beside the Trash icon, making it easy to drag and delete accidentally. It's a common mistake among Mac users.

Although you can access downloaded files through Finder, pulling it up every time is inconvenient. So, here are three things you can do to bring a missing Downloads folder back to your Mac's Dock.

1. Check Your Trash

Before anything else, check your Trash. See if you also deleted the contents of your Downloads folder. The Put Back function is the quickest way to recover erased data on your Mac, although it can't restore Dock shortcuts.

To do this, open Trash, Control-click on the Downloads folder if you see it, and choose Put Back.

2. Add Downloads to Dock From the Finder Sidebar

The only way to access a missing Downloads folder is through Finder in macOS. You should see it on the sidebar under Favorites.

Right-click Downloads from there and choose Add to Dock or drag Downloads from the sidebar straight to your Mac's Dock. Both actions yield the same result.

3. Manually Search for the Downloads Folder

If your downloads folder is missing from the Finder sidebar, manually search for it through Finder first.

Launch Finder, then use the Search bar in the top-right corner of the Window to look for Downloads. Now, drag the appropriate Downloads folder to the sidebar. Afterward, just Control-click Downloads and choose Add to Dock.

Check the directory path of the Downloads folder you drag. The local folder shows Users > Account Name > Downloads , whereas the iCloud Drive folder shows iCloud > Downloads . You're supposed to drag the former.

Manage Your Mac's Folders Efficiently

Don't ignore the missing Downloads folder in your Mac's Dock. Testing all the above troubleshooting tips will take you less than five minutes.

Also, organize the other folders you frequently use. Creating shortcuts in your Mac's Dock, desktop, and sidebar will save you the hassle of opening Finder.
