Ali Arouzi is a journalist who has gained fame for extraordinary journalism for the last ten years and has carved out an extraordinary professional path. Arouzi is working as an NBC Tehran bureau chief and correspondent. He covers major news stories on Iran and the Middle East. He is most recognized for his work on Dateline NBC (1992), Living Dangerously (2003), and Ann Curry Reports President of Iran Speaks (2013).

Early Life

Ali Arouzi was born in Tehran in 1974 and grew up in an Iranian family. Julius Arouzi and Rosalie Arouzi are his parents. He has a brother, Tony. He finished his high school graduation from a school in Shiraz, and then he pursued his passion for journalism at the University of Tehran.

Ali Arouzi Career

Ali’s career began as a reporter for Iranian news agency, IRNA, and gradually he became a news anchor. He used to be a very famous reporter in Iran. After he relocated to the US he worked with renowned news organizations like CNN and Fox News. A television program he produced, Living Dangerously, aired in 2003. A 31-episode documentary television series was involved.

He again returned to Iran in 2009. He became a news anchor on Press TV, a state-run television network. After this, his career took a significant turn and there was no coming back. His balanced reporting and unwavering commitment to objectivity earned him appreciation.
