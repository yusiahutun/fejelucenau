How to Stream &quot;The Ballad of Songbirds and Snakes&quot;

Publish date: 2024-09-23

Fans will finally get the chance to venture back into the Hunger Games cinematic universe with the prequel film, The Ballad of Songbirds and Snakes, which officially releases in theaters today. But, for those who prefer to return to Panem in the comfort of their own home, they'll have to wait a few more months until it's available on streaming.

The Ballad of Songbirds and Snakes, based off of the 2020 Suzanne Collins novel of the same name, stars Tom Blyth as a young Coriolanus Snow, Panem's future dictator, and Rachel Zegler as Lucy Gray Baird, the erased District 12 victor of the 10th annual Hunger Games.

The official synopsis reads:

THE HUNGER GAMES: THE BALLAD OF SONGBIRDS & SNAKES follows a young Coriolanus (Tom Blyth) who is the last hope for his failing lineage, the once-proud Snow family that has fallen from grace in a post-war Capitol. With his livelihood threatened, Snow is reluctantly assigned to mentor Lucy Gray Baird (Rachel Zegler), a tribute from the impoverished District 12. Read ahead to find out how and when you can stream the film.

Where can I stream it?

The prequel movie will more than likely land on Peacock, following Lionsgate's multiyear deal with the streaming service, according to Collider.

Peacock offers different packages for customers, with the most popular options starting at $5.99 per month or $59.99 per year. Also included in their premium streaming package are the ability to tune into live sports and events, current shows on NBC and Bravo, and more than 50 channels.

When will be available for streaming?

The official release date has yet to be announced, though we can most likely expect to see The Ballad of Songbirds and Snakes hit the streaming service in 2024, which is when Lionsgate's deal with Peacock goes into effect.
