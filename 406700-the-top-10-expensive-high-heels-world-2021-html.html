The top 10 most expensive high heels in the world in 2021

Publish date: 2024-02-08

When talking about luxurious high heels, what probably comes to your mind is the story of Cinderella – how her simple life was turned into a luxurious one overnight. The story was sweet, but the heels she wore that night were magical. High heels look stylish and are available in different attractive colours. Which are the most expensive high heels in the world?

When it comes to beauty and fashion, women are known to lead the way. Right from the hair and earrings till down the heels, they never disappoint. While shoes might be a basic need for most people, others take serious considerations of what they put on their feet.

Most expensive high heels

Most women spend thousands of dollars each year buying shoes. Here is a list of the top 10 most expensive high heels in the world:

1. Ruby Slippers by House of Harry Winston - $3.01 million

At the top of the list is the Ruby Slippers by House of Harry Winston. The heels are made from precious stones and quality leather. They were once featured in the film Wizard of OZ and worn by Dorothy Gale.

2. Stuart Weitzman Rita Hayworth - $3 million

Rita Hayworth's favourite earrings inspired Stuart Weitzman Rita Hayworth. The heels were made by a collaboration between Stuart Weitzman and Eddie LeVian, resulting in a rust-coloured satin, rubies, diamonds, and sapphires. Kathleen Bird first wore them in the 2006 Academy awards.

3. Tanzanite Heels by Stuart Weitzman - $2 million

The Tanzanite heels by Stuart Weitzman are fitted with 185-carats of rare purple-blue Tanzanite gemstones from Africa. This makes them one of the most decorated heels in the world. Apart from the look, they also fetch a whopping two million dollars in market price. The heels are manufactured upon order by the potential client.

4. Stuart Weitzman Cinderella Slippers - $2 million

The Cinderella slippers by Stuart Weitzman are adorned with 565 rare diamonds, which weigh about 55-carts. They also feature a 5-carat Amaretto Diamond, which is valued at above a million dollars on its own. All these features make it the best high heels in the world.

5. Stuart Weitzman Ruby Slippers - $1.6 million

Stuart Weitzman slippers are one of the most expensive heels brands in the world. The brand is a product of ingenuity between two houses that partnered to deliver a masterpiece to the world of fashion. It is decorated with 123.33-carat diamonds and completed with 642 Burmese rubies, which are rare to find.

6. Stuart Weitzman Platinum Guild stiletto - $1.2 million

Stuart Weitzman Platinum Guild is some of the most expensive stilettos in the world. Those who opt to buy it go for the quality of stitching, inspiring jewels and precious stones used to design it. Laura Harring wore the shoes during the 2002 Academy Awards.

7. Stuart Weitzman Marilyn Monroe shoes - $1.1 million

Marilyn Monroe is a unique pair of heels as Hollywood stars, and fashion icons inspired it. The pair is fitted with Swarovski Crystal earrings at heart as a signature design. The crystals are surrounded by satin rose, which breeds a sense of glamour and high status.

8. Stuart Weitzman Retro Rose pumps - $1 million

Retro Rose is one of the most expensive yet most sought-after pair of heels. It is adorned with 1800 Kwait diamonds weighing 100-carat and 400 shimmering diamonds to complete its uniqueness. They may not be the most expensive stilettos, but this is a price only a few can afford.

9. Ruby Slippers from the Wizard Of Oz - $612,000

Some of the most expensive women heels come from brands never heard of by many such as Ruby Slippers. The high heels are made from exotic materials such as red sequins and chiffon to complete the already comfy interior.

10. Stuart Weitzman Diamond Dream stiletto - $500,000

This pair of heels was designed by a collaboration between the renowned shoemaker Stuart Weitzman and Kwait. The diamond jewellery kings saw the stilettos adorned with 1,420 diamonds weighing 30-carats. Only a few pairs made on-demand every year, with those owning it even considering it an investment.
