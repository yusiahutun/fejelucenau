Originating from Ancient Greece

The Small Greek Domestic Dog, also known as the Greek Canis, can trace its roots back to Ancient Greece. This breed has been a beloved companion for centuries, known for its loyalty and affectionate nature.

Compact and Agile

These dogs are small in size, typically weighing between 12 to 20 pounds. Despite their small stature, they are incredibly agile and athletic, making them excellent for various dog sports and activities.

Intelligent and Easy to Train

The Small Greek Domestic Dog is highly intelligent and eager to please. They are known for their ability to quickly learn commands and tricks, making them easy to train for obedience and agility competitions.

Great Watchdogs

Don’t let their small size fool you! These dogs may be compact, but they have a strong protective instinct. They are alert and vigilant, making them excellent watchdogs that will alert their owners to any potential dangers.

Social and Friendly

The Greek Canis is known for its friendly and sociable nature. They get along well with children and other pets, making them a great addition to any family setting.

Low Maintenance Grooming

One of the perks of owning a Small Greek Domestic Dog is that they have a short and easy-to-maintain coat. Occasional brushing and regular bathing will keep their coat healthy and shiny.

Long Lifespan

These dogs have an impressive lifespan, with some individuals living up to 16 years or more. Proper nutrition, exercise, and regular veterinary care contribute to their longevity.

Excellent Travel Companions

Due to their small size and adaptable nature, Small Greek Domestic Dogs make great travel companions. They can easily accompany their owners on road trips and flights, making them the perfect pet for adventurous individuals.

Versatile Working Dogs

Despite their reputation as companion animals, Small Greek Domestic Dogs also excel in various working roles. They can be trained as therapy dogs, search and rescue dogs, and even excel in canine sports such as agility and obedience.

Active and Energetic

Adaptability to Different Environments

Small Greek Domestic Dogs are adaptable to different living environments, whether it’s a compact apartment or a spacious house with a backyard. As long as they receive proper exercise and mental stimulation, they can thrive in various settings.

Strong Bonds with Their Owners

These dogs are known for their unwavering loyalty and strong bonds with their owners. They form deep connections and will always be by your side, providing love and companionship.

Good Health and Few Genetic Health Issues

Small Greek Domestic Dogs generally enjoy good health and are prone to fewer genetic health issues compared to some other dog breeds. However, regular vet check-ups and a nutritious diet are still essential to ensure their well-being.

Preservation Efforts

Due to their historical significance and unique characteristics, there are ongoing efforts to preserve and promote the Small Greek Domestic Dog breed. These efforts include breeding programs and raising awareness about their importance in Greek culture.

FAQs

1. How small is the Greek domestic dog?

The Greek domestic dog is a small-sized breed, typically weighing between 10 to 15 pounds and standing about 10 to 12 inches tall at the shoulder.

2. Are Greek domestic dogs good with children?

Yes, Greek domestic dogs are known to be good with children. They are generally friendly, affectionate, and patient, making them great companions for families.

3. Do Greek domestic dogs require a lot of exercise?

While Greek domestic dogs are active and energetic, they do not require excessive exercise. Regular walks, playtime, and mental stimulation should be sufficient to keep them happy and healthy.

4. Are Greek domestic dogs easy to train?

Yes, Greek domestic dogs are intelligent and eager to please, making them relatively easy to train. Consistent positive reinforcement techniques and socialization from an early age will help in their training process.

5. Do Greek domestic dogs shed a lot?

Greek domestic dogs have a short and dense coat, which means they don’t shed excessively. However, regular grooming sessions and brushing can help control shedding and keep their coat in good condition.

6. Are Greek domestic dogs suitable for apartment living?

Absolutely! Greek domestic dogs are adaptable and can do well in apartment living conditions. However, they will require regular exercise and mental stimulation to keep them happy and content.

7. Are Greek domestic dogs prone to any health issues?

Overall, Greek domestic dogs are a healthy breed with no major breed-specific health concerns. However, as with any dog, routine veterinary care and a healthy lifestyle are essential to their overall well-being.
