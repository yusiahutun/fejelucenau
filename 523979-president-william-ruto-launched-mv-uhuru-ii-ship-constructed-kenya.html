MV Uhuru II: William Ruto Launches Kenyas First Locally Built Ship in Kisumu

Publish date: 2024-02-17
  • President William Ruto on Monday, October 9, launched the first locally constructed ship in Kenya and East Africa at large
  • Ruto praised former president Uhuru Kenyatta for his foresight in coming up with the shipyard refurbishment
  • The ship has an impressive capacity of 1,800 tons and was crafted at the Kenya Ship Yard through a collaborative effort between Kenyan agencies and a Dutch firm

On Monday, October 9, President William Ruto commissioned the MV Uhuru II at the Kenya Shipyards Limited in Kisumu.

MV Uhuru II is the first locally constructed ship in Kenya, making the country a pioneer in the shipbuilding industry.

Why William Ruto congratulated Uhuru during Kisumu tour

During the event, the head of state appreciated former president Uhuru Kenyatta for his input and all the professionals who skillfully exhibited their admirable professionalism in their work.

"I hereby place MV Uhuru II into commission. May her engines roar and bring her to life. May the glide of her sail be smooth. May good tidings be with her and her crew. One morning he asked me for us to visit Kisumu and see what the military could do, when we came here it was a heap of dirt, wreckage and old buildings," said Ruto.

The president noted that the Kenya shipyard is positioned to become Kenya's strategic anchor in the development of the country's blue economy and all the resources associated with it.

“To see what we are seeing here is phenomenal and I must say both President Kenyatta and the military’s leadership, with a lot of foresight drove this program to where we are today,” he added.

Consequently, the Kenya shipyard has been contracted to repair and maintain ships for the government's ministries, departments and agencies, including the Kenya Coast Guard Services, the Kenya Ports Authority, the Kenya Maritime Authority, the Kenya Marine Research Institute, and private operators in the Indian Ocean and Lake victoria.

Who built the MV Uhuru II

With a capacity of 1,800 tons, MV Uhuru II was built at the Kenya Shipyard by Kenyan agencies, including the Kenya Defense Forces, in partnership with a Dutch firm, Damen Shipyards.

The ship boasts an impressive capacity of 1,800 tons.

The 100-meter ship can transport 22 wagons across the lake and has two million litres of crude oil capacity per voyage.

Brigadier Paul Otieno Owuor, the managing director of the Kenya Shipyard, said that the ship can reach nearby countries Tanzania and Uganda within 10 hours.
