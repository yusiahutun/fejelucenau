Publish date: 2024-05-15

Toner is one of the most mysterious skincare products we’ve encountered as beauty editors. In the past, toners were harsh, powerful solutions that made your skin overly dry and stripped it of its natural oils. Today, luckily, toners come in gentler options, made for every need from sensitive skin to mature skin and everything in between.

However, to the layperson, toner can still be pretty confusing. To make it easier for you to figure out what toner is best for you, we tested 24 of the most popular options out there and rated them carefully on consistency, feel, active ingredients, and results. What to Keep in Mind

Skin Type

When looking for a toner (or any skincare product for that matter), it’s important to find a product that caters to your specific skin type. “The right toner may differ from person to person depending on the skin type,” explains Dr. Dendy Engelman, board-certified cosmetic dermatologist. “If you have oily or acne-prone skin, you may want to look for a toner that contains both gentle exfoliants and soothing ingredients to balance the skin and prevent breakouts without over-drying.” (We recommend Farmacy Deep Sweep Pore Cleaning Toner)

“If you have dry or sensitive skin, you should look for gentle toners that have a calming, hydrating effect on skin. Botanicals like aloe vera and cucumber will help soothe and cool vulnerable skin. I recommend witch hazel for these skin types, too,” adds Dr. Engelman For those with dry or sensitive skin, Laneige Cream Skin Toner & Moisturizer is a great option because it deeply hydrates and soothes complexions.

The bottom line: Finding the right toner for your skin type is essential since it will not only help address your skincare concerns, but it will also be safer for your skin.

Ingredients

Beyond recognizing your skin type, you should also consider what ingredients your skin needs. “If you have dry or sensitive skin, you can find a toner with hyaluronic acid to use after cleansing,” says board-certified dermatologist and dermatologic surgeon Dr. Dustin Portela. (Anyone with dry skin should consider using fresh Rose & Hyaluronic Acid Deep Hydration Toner, AbsoluteJOI Balancing pH Skin Toner with Aloe, innisfree Green Tea Hyaluronic Acid Hydrating Toner, or Fenty Beauty Fat Water Hydrating Milky Toner Essence with Hyaluronic Acid + Tamarind.)

On the other hand, if your skin is blemish-prone, look for a toner that has salicylic acid, which can tone down inflamed skin. (Opt for Farmacy Deep Sweep Pore Cleaning Toner or SK-II Facial Treatment Clear Lotion Toner in this case.) Additionally, if you want to treat hyperpigmentation, seek out a toner that includes lactic acid because this ingredient works to exfoliate skin and fade dark spots. (You can try Ren Clean Skincare Ready Steady Glow Daily AHA Tonic and Freck Beauty Cactus Water Cleansing Lactic Acid Toner)

On the flip side, there are also ingredients to avoid. “In general and in facial toners, you want to steer clear of products that contain fragrances or alcohol that might irritate the skin,” says Dr. Engelman. “Any synthetic dyes, parabens, or essential oils can also cause irritation, over-drying, or inflammation of the skin.” Be sure to read the label of every beauty product you’re buying to ensure your skin is protected against potential damage.

Your Questions, Answered

What is facial toner?

Essentially, face toner is a skincare product that’s used to cleanse, hydrate, soothe, and tone complexions. “Think of toner as the ‘prep-step,’ the first step after cleansing, which should prep the skin for the remainder of your skincare routine,” says Jordan Harper, nurse practitioner and founder of Barefaced. (Toner is used after you wash your face and before you apply serums or moisturizers.)

What does facial toner do?

“Facial toners were originally formulated to balance the skin's pH after cleansing,” explains Hallie Font, director of esthetics at Hi, skin. “Now that cleansers have evolved and are generally more pH balanced, toners are more often used to prep, hydrate, and nourish the skin after cleansing and before applying serums and moisturizer.”

Additionally, they can be used to remove any residual cleanser, makeup, or dirt from the face. Depending on which toner you pick, you can also address additional skincare concerns such as hyperpigmentation, dryness, redness, and more.

Is face toner necessary?

“While it shouldn't be the highest priority in your skincare arsenal, toners do offer significant benefits, provide real results, and enhance your serums and moisturizer,” says Font. So, if you’re a skin minimalist, you can skip this step if you have other products that cater to your skin’s needs. However, if you really want to address certain skincare issues quickly and effectively, toner can definitely speed that process up.

When should I use toner?

“Face toner is best used after cleansing your skin, and before using serums or lotions in your routine,” says Dr. Portela. “Most toners are safe to use daily, and those that contain hydrating ingredients can even be used twice daily if you desire.” (If your skin needs a boost of hydration, we suggest bringing the Tower28 SOS Daily Rescue Facial Spray with you on the go, since you can easily spritz it on your face when needed.)

Can I use toner every day?

“In most cases, it is safe to use a toner every day,” explains Dr. Portela. However, it’s important to take your skin type into account when it comes to using toner. “Whether or not toner is recommended for daily use depends on one’s skin type,” explains Dr. Engleman. “Some people can use toner every day, but people with extremely sensitive or dry skin (or certain skin conditions) may experience irritation and over-drying with daily toner use. If any of those descriptions apply to you, talk to your dermatologist about what toner is right for you and how often you should use it.”

But, generally, it is safe to use toner once or twice a day. If you are using a hydrating toner, feel free to use it twice a day. If you are using a toner with acids, start by initially introducing it into your routine two to three times a week to see how your skin responds. This way you won’t dry out your skin or harm your skin’s moisture barrier.
