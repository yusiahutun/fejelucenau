How Much Is Bobby Wooten III Net Worth?

Bobby Wooten III has built an accumulated net worth of $750K.

First and foremost, Bobby is a musician, a bassist to be more precise. He claimed in an interview with Bass Magazine, that he began playing bass around the same time that his pals became fans of pop-punk icons Blink-182. His friends requested him to play bass, and that is how he began playing the instrument.

Bobby stated he rapidly learned numerous Motown songs, as well as other favorites like Stevie Wonder and the Red Hot Chili Peppers, once he got started. According to AllMusic, his music credit includes Carly Rae Jepsen, Machine Gun Kelly, and David Byrne.

Besides his collaboration with musicians, Bobby has also performed on broadway. He was a member of a touring band for the extensive American Utopia tour and its subsequent run on Broadway. He also performed on the live album that came out of the Broadway performances, and he performed as a part of the show when David was the musical guest on Saturday Night Live.

While interviewing with Bass in December 2021, Bobby opened up about the great impact the show had. He explained, “In American Utopia, [David] very much wants everyone to have their own voice. That’s why there are different characters and each person’s spirit resonates differently. It’s a great thing to be performing on Broadway as yourself”.

He added further, “What I’ve taken away from working with him is he likes things to be simple: to put forward an idea and not distract from it, which is awesome”.

Moreover, Bobby has also received credit as the bass player on the cast album for the stage adaptation of Moulin Rouge! The Musical. 

According to Bobby’s Facebook, he studied Jazz at Indiana University (Bloomington) and graduated in the class of 2011.

Per his LinkedIn which is likely Bobby’s, he is a self-employed professional musician.

Bobby Wooten III Family

Bobby Wooten III was born to his father Bobby Wooten II and mother Ismadi Hernandez Wooten. While speaking with PEOPLE, Bobby mentioned his parents and grandparents.

“When I was around 5 years old, my grandfather wanted to step down from leading the choir, so we moved out to Chicago so that my dad could take it over,” he told the outlet. “I played keys and bass in the church every week. I never took a bass lesson until I got to college, which was more about learning upright bass. I’m not really actively going to church now, but if I’m making music on a Sunday, I always find I start with gospel music — just playing it for myself. It’s very much a part of me.”

In his profile on NYU, where he works as an adjunct professor, Bobby has credited his family’s gospel Wooten Choral Ensemble as his main influence & inspiration.

His mother Ismadi Hernandez Wooten is studying at Douglass College (Rutgers University). She is a graduate of Archbishop Walsh High School, Irvington, NJ.
