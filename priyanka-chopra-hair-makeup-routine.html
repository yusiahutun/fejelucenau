Priyanka Chopra Little Black Book Episode 3

Publish date: 2024-06-09

Priyanka Chopra entered the beauty pageant circuit at the age of 17—so she knows a thing or two about beauty. But nothing prepared her for the constant glam time that comes with being an actress. "I literally spend about two hours every single day in the hair and makeup chair," she says.

In episode three of Chopra's BAZAAR series Little Black Book, she let's us follow along as she gets her hair and makeup done for the 2018 Met Gala. Chopra works with makeup artist Pati Dubroff, whose other clients include Margot Robbie and Natalie Portman. "Whatever problems I might be having on my skin, Pati knows how to take care of it," notes Chopra.

Shop Priyanka's Beauty Look

Dubroff is a master at prepping skin before makeup. First, she starts with the 111Skin sheet masks. "They give this incredible plump glow, and they just feel good," notes the makeup artist. Chopra adds: "It was cold as hell. She put it on my face and I literally screamed."

After peeling off the mask, Dubroff likes to massage Chopra's face with a roller. "These are Japanese face tools and they help to stimulate circulation and move fluids around," she says.

But it's Dubroff's moisturizer trick that might be the biggest game-changer."Moisturizer is important but you don't put it everywhere," she says. Dubroff only pats moisturizer on where Chopra needs it—the outer edges of her face—then uses a mattifying primer along the t-zone and center.

There is one feature Chopra won't let Dubroff touch: her eyebrows. The actress prefers to do them herself. "I change my eyebrows according to whatever character I want," she says. "You can absolutely change what your face looks like with what you do with your eyebrows."

Chopra loves makeup, but hair wasn't always her thing. "My hair was my biggest problem. It went from being my biggest problem to my crowning glory," she notes. The actress, who works with Pantene as an ambassador, believes good hair starts in the shower. "It's so important to find the right shampoo and conditioner for your hair. Deep mask, twice a week, at least. I love oiling my hair with coconut oil for the scalp. It allows for circulation which is amazing," she says.
