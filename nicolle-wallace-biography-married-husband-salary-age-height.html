For Nicolle Wallace, an accomplished author, American political commentator, and chief policy analyst for NBC and MSNBC News, her world seems to be very perfect. Nicolle is the current host of Deadline: The White House. She is a regular guest presenter as well as a contributor to NBC’s Today show and The 11th Hour with Brian Williams and Morning Joe – both MSNBC programs. The television personality also acted as a defense attorney and spokesperson for the campaign and appeared regularly on cable television and network news programs. Nicolle is also the author of the New York Times’ and It’s Classified’s best-selling modern political novel Eighteen Acres. In 2015, her third novel entitled Madam President was published.

Nicolle Wallace Biography, Age

She was named Nicolle Devenish after her birth on February 4, 1972, in Orange County, Southern California, United States. She was born as the first child of a third-grade public school teacher (mother) and her husband, who earned his living as an antique dealer. She spent her formative years with three siblings in Orinda, located in northern California. Her family is originally from England, as her grandfather Thomas Devenish was born in England and later immigrated with his family to the United States in 1947. Thomas was the one who founded the antique store that Nicolle’s father runs today.

An examination of her academic background revealed that she received her early education at Miramonte High School, which she graduated from in 1990. After graduating from high school, Nicolle Wallace went on to the University of California at Berkeley, where she earned a Bachelor of Arts degree in Mass Communications in 1994. The aspirations of the budding journalist for higher qualifications at the time led her to the Medill School of Journalism at Northwestern University, where she earned a Master’s degree in 1996. It is important to note that her parents were very supportive of her academic and professional aspirations. No wonder she was so successful in such a short time.
