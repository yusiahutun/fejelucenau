Detroit boy missing nearly two weeks after 'being hidden in his dad's basement by cruel stepmother'

Publish date: 2024-02-21

Little Charlie Bothuell is gradually getting over the ordeal of apparently being locked in a basement by an abusive stepmother, with his real mother’s family exclusively telling MailOnline, ‘He’s laughing again’, in their first interview.

Since Charlie was dramatically found by police on Wednesday, 11 days after going missing and while his father Charles Bothuell IV was giving a live TV interview, his family have remained tight-lipped.

He was immediately taken away from his father and stepmom, Monique Dillard-Bothuell, amid claims of abuse, and given to his biological mother Africa Shippings, where he has stayed hidden.

Found: Charlie Bothuell V was found hiding in his father's basement after he'd been missing for 11 days

But yesterday 12-year-old Charlie’s aunt Alicia Underwood broke the family’s silence and spoke exclusively to MailOnline, to give the heart-warming news, ‘He’s laughing again, to get that smile is the best thing ever.’

Speaking from her home in Southfield, Michigan a few miles from downtown Detroit, where Charlie originally went missing and is now staying, Africa’s sister Alicia, went on: ‘We are so happy that he is home with us and that he is gradually getting back to normal.

‘It will take time, but with the love of his family he will get there.’

The boy told police that Monique Dillard-Bothuell barricaded him behind boxes and told him 'not to come out, no matter what he hears,' according to court documents filed after she was arrested Thursday.

Charlie's grandmother told the FBI 'he was very skinny, and almost looked like a cancer patient. He had marks all over his arms and chest' the last time she saw him.'

These latest developments led authorities to remove the couple's two younger children from their care and place them into protective custody.

Since his discovery, Charlie’s stepmom has been arrested on gun charges, her two younger children have been taken into care and Charles IV has not been able to see his son as police have taken him to live with his mother.

Despite apparently telling investigators that his stepmom ordered him to hide in the cramped basement beneath their condo, Charlie is yet to open up to his family about his ordeal.

Alicia, 38, explained: ‘I have spoken to him a lot, but he doesn’t speak of what happened to him. We are just surrounding him with his family and friends and trying to keep him laughing so he can gradually get back to normal.

‘When he feels comfortable and ready he will tell us what really happened. At the moment he is playing computer games… and he loves superhero films, so today we are going to watch Transformers and The Avengers and all that other good stuff, to give him a sense of normalcy.

‘… We just hope he can go on to live a normal life.'

And she had strong words for her nephew's alleged abusers, adding: 'If his stepmom or his biological father are found guilty of the things that are being said, we want them to be prosecuted through the courts.

'I would ask people to pray for them, because if they did this, they really need God's help'.

Charlie’s disappearance and dramatic discovery have gripped the nation. Since he was found on Wednesday a number of reports of shocking abuse have surfaced.

He was home-schooled and his dad apparently kept incredibly strict education and exercise regimes, with NBC News reporting he was made to complete 4,000 strides on an elliptical machine every day.

Yesterday even more worrying allegations emerged, with The Detroit Free Press reporting that court documents allege Charlie had a scar caused by his father 'driving a PVC pipe into his chest as punishment for a transgression’.

Police sources told WJBK a bloody pipe was found in the home, and 'Mr. Bothuell disclosed physically disciplining Charlie V with [it],' police said. Blood was also apparently found on his clothes.

The blood has not immediately been explained, but the reportedly abusive father's lawyer claimed to police it was from eczema. 'He scratched it like crazy,' said attorney Mark Magidson.

Court documents also accuse stepmom, Dillard-Bothuell of barricading Charlie behind boxes and telling him 'not to come out, no matter what he hears,'

Missing: The townhouse in Detroit where Charlie Bothuell V was found alive on June 25

Escape: The door at the end of the tunnel - at the bottom of the stairs - was open all last week because people were putting their garbage in a dumpster. That means Charlie could have come and gone as he pleased. It is usually locked from the outside

Alicia says her family had no idea Charlie was suffering any kind of abuse, but if the allegations are true, she wants Bothuell and his wife to be severely punished.

She said: ‘We want justice to be served... We had absolutely no idea anything like this was going on and if we did, as a family, we would have taken the necessary steps. We are a very tight-knit family.

‘Charlie has never said anything about any kind of abuse, but most abused kids don’t say anything. Now at least he is in an environment where he can feel safe enough to speak about it and tell the truth.’

Since the story broke many people have asked why Charlie was living with his father and not his biological mother. Alicia explained: ‘He has always been a good boy, but his grades were declining and his mother wanted to nip it in the bud and get him back on the right track.

‘She felt maybe spending more quality time with his dad might help him out. It was a decision they made together. Her only aim was to help Charlie.’

Charlie was discovered in the cramped basement behind a makeshift barrier of boxes. Confusingly, FBI agents previously searched the basement, even using specially trained cadaver dogs. It then emerged there was an underground tunnel easily accessible from the basement, connecting the other condos in the block and leading outside the building.

On Thursday MailOnline published exclusive pictures of the tunnel, including a storage area, which neighbors believe he could have hidden in. The tunnel even has a bathroom.

One neighbor said the pre-teen had been spotted out playing basketball in the neighbourhood while law enforcement and family carried out their desperate search.

The tunnel’s outside door is usually locked and only accessible to maintenance men, but crucially last week, it was apparently open so residents could get rid of a build-up of trash in a dumpster left outside.

That means Charlie could have left the property in the day, before returning at night, explaining why FBI agents could not find the boy.

Court documents apparently state that Charlie has claimed he even snuck upstairs for food when the house was empty.   

Speaking to the MailOnline, the neighbor, who did not want to be named, said: ‘As far as I am concerned this kid ran away, then he was found and that’s it.

‘He ran away two years ago, that has been in the news, so it doesn’t take a genius to work out that the most obvious explanation here is that he ran away again.’

Despite the neighbor’s assertions, a huge amount of suspicion surrounds Charlie’s father and stepmom, with many suggesting he would not have been able to stay hidden without an adult’s involvement.

Monique Dillard-Bothuell was arrested on Thursday after apparently violating parole relating to a previous arrest on a gun charge.

Authorities had obtained a warrant for Monique Bothuell's arrest on Monday, but held out until Thursday to take her into custody later claiming the search for Charlie took priority.

Arrest: Monique Dillard-Bothuell is arraigned for a probation violation of weapons charges at Frank Murphy Hall of Justice in Detroit on Friday, two days after her stepson was found in her basement

Back with mom: Charlie is now staying with his birth mother

Stunned: Charlie Bothuell IV was shocked into silence on Wednesday when HLN's Nancy Grace told him his 12-year-old son had been found alive in his basement, 11 days after he vanished

The 37-year-old had pleaded guilty to purchasing a pistol without a permit earlier this year and had been put on a two-year probation period.

Following Charlie’s disappearance on June 14th, Charles IV and Charlie’s biological mother Africa Shippings who lives around two miles away both made emotional television pleas for their boy’s safe return. Apparently Charlie would split his time between his father’s and his mother’s.

Charles IV was on live television on HLN with Nancy Grace on Wednesday when he learned that his son had been found in the home's basement.

'We're getting reports that your son has been found in your basement,' Grace said.

As the shocked father struggled to comprehend the news - grabbing his chest and looking stunned - Grace demanded to know whether or not he had checked his basement.

Overwhelmed: Bothuell clutched his chest and breathed heavily after learning that his son had been found

Relieved: The boy's father was so emotional when his son was discovered that he hugged a reporter

'I checked my basement,' Bothuell said. 'The FBI checked my basement. The police checked my basement. My wife checked my basement. I've been down there several times. We've all been checking.'

Detroit Police Chief James Craig said when police found the boy, he appeared to be hiding behind a makeshift barrier of boxes and did not announce himself.

Craig said that he believes it is unlikely that the boy could have constructed the barrier himself. He was also behind a large plastic drum and bedding was found nearby.

'There's no way he could have erected this make-shift area of concealment, I'll call it,' Craig told WJBK. 'He certainly was excited to see us. I had a chance to actually talk and embrace Charlie.

'He indicated he was hungry. He appeared fine.'

Craig told reporters earlier Wednesday that the boy's father had taken a polygraph test about his son's disappearance, but the boy's stepmother declined to do so.

Mr Bothuell's test came back inconclusive, WDIV reported.

He also told authorities that Charlie had ran away from home two years ago, but police found him after three hours.
