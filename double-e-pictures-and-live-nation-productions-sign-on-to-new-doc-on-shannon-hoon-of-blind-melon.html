Double E Pictures And Live Nation Productions Sign On To New Doc About Blind Melon Frontman Shannon

Publish date: 2024-08-31

LOS ANGELES (CelebrityAccess) – Double E Pictures and Live Nation Productions have announced today (Nov. 21) that they have come on-board as executive producers of the award-winning documentary, All I Can Say.

The film, which is an intimate account of the final five years of Blind Melon frontman Shannon Hoon’s life, was co-directed by Grammy® award-nominated filmmakers, Danny Clinch, Taryn Gould, and Colleen Hennessy; produced by Lindha Narvaez, Samuel Gursky, and Taryn Gould; and executive produced by Eric Eisner (Long Strange Trip), founder and CEO of Double E Pictures, Michael Rapino, CEO of Live Nation Entertainment, Ryan Kroft, Senior Vice President of Production & Development for Live Nation Productions, Danny Clinch and John Beug.

The doc, which has received accolades at multiple festivals including the 2019 Tribeca Film Festival, New Hampshire Film Festival (winner of best feature documentary), and Sound Unseen Festival (winner of the grand jury prize), was created using the Hoon’s own footage, voice, and music, and provides an inside look into his family, his creative process, his band’s rise to fame, and his struggle with addiction, among other things.

All I Can Say will make its international premiere at the International Documentary Film Festival in Amsterdam on November 22, 2020.
