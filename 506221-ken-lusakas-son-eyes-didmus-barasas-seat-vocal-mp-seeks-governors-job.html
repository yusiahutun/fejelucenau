Bungoma - Bungoma governor Kenneth Lusaka’s son Duncan Aura Makelo has given a strong hint that he is considering joining the world of politics.

Speculations have been rife for the past year that the firstborn son of the former Senate Speaker is eyeing the Kimilili parliamentary seat.

The seat is currently held by vocal MP Didmus Barasa. Barasa, who is serving his second term in office has indicated that he is targeting the Bungoma gubernatorial seat in 2027.

Duncan Aura Makelo almost makes his intention known

Aura, an advocate by profession with a bias in Commercial and Tax law during the launch of an 8-classroom-story building at Kimilili RC Girls Primary School, fell short of officially declaring his interest for the seat but instead chose to heap praise on the area MP who was present at the well-attended event.

“As residents of Kimilili constituency, we are very happy with your development. When we elevate you in 2027, your seat should be left in the hands of a person who will continue with your good work. That person can be your fellow young person,” he told the area legislator.

The younger Makelo is not new to politics, having played a behind-the-scenes critical role in marshalling up the support and votes of young people for his father as governor in the three previous elections.

Younger Makelo has touch with ground

Aura has however been increasingly visible over the past five months, where he has been traversing Kimilili constituency and actively participating in public events such as fundraisers and funerals.

In November 2022, Barasa was elated after the petition challenging his win in the August 9 poll was dismissed.

Brian Khaemba suffers significant blow

In her ruling, Justice Mary Kasango of the High Court in Bungoma said the petitioner, Brian Khaemba, had failed to show how the malpractice interfered with the rights of the electorate.

Khaemba had based his petition on alleged violence witnessed during the election.

Kasango ordered Khaemba to pay KSh 2.7 million as part of the costs. Barasa was set to receive KSh 1.1 million, while Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) and the Kimilili Returning Officer were to pocket KSh 0.8 million each.
