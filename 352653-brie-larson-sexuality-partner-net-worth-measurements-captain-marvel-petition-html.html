Brie Larson was born on October 1, 1989, in Sacramento, California, to her parents, Heather and Sylvain Desaulniers. Her real name is Brianne Sidonie Desaulniers. Her parents worked together as homoeopathic chiropractors.

Larson was brought up together with her younger sister, Milaine. Instead of going for formal education at nearby schools, she was home-schooled.

At six years old, Brie opened up about her dream of being an actress, and her parents wasted no time to help her join an acting training program at the American Conservatory Theater. From then, she honed her acting skills in readiness for major acting roles in the film industry.

Career

Larson went into acting from an early age of only eight years. In 1998, she appeared on TV shows, The Tonight Show with Jay Leno and To Have & to Hold. After her debut on TV, she went on to portray different characters in several other TV shows.

In 2004, she played minor roles in the films 13 Going on 30 and Sleepover. Her breakthrough in movies came in 2006 when she landed significant roles in the teen comedy Hoot.

In 2015, Larson landed a career-defining role when she played Joy in the drama movie Room. Her exceptional performance in the movie helped her win the Academy Award for the Best Actress. She also bagged the Golden Globe, BAFTA, and Screen Actors Guild Awards.

Advocacy

Larson is also known for gender equality activism and support for sexual assault survivors. As a celebrity, she uses her platforms to talk about political and social matters affecting the most vulnerable people.

In 2014, Larson teamed up with Tina Poppy and Alia Penner to form Women of Cinefamily support group. Women of Cinefamily is a non-profit program focusing on films directed and starred by women.

Captain Marvel petition

An online petition has been on for quite some time, demanding that Larson relinquish her role as Carol Danvers in Captain Marvel. Some fans and critics feel that she is not fit for the role and would want it to be taken by another character, preferably Monica Rambeau.

Brie Larson controversy

Her remarks were hugely misinterpreted to mean that she hates white males, and it led to internet trolls as well as negative reviews on her role in Captain Marvel.
