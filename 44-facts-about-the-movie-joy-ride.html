Joy Ride was released in 2001.

Directed by John Dahl, the movie Joy Ride is a thrilling suspense film that captivated audiences upon its release in Starring Paul Walker, Steve Zahn, and Leelee Sobieski, the film tells the story of three friends who embark on a cross-country road trip that takes a terrifying turn.

The movie was originally titled “Squelch”.

Did you know that Joy Ride was initially titled “Squelch”? The name was later changed to Joy Ride to reflect the thrill and excitement of the film’s storyline.

Paul Walker’s character is named Lewis Thomas.

In the movie, Paul Walker portrays the character of Lewis Thomas, a young man who rents a car with his brother to embark on a road trip. Little did he know that the seemingly innocent decision would lead them into a nightmare.

The antagonist is known as “Rusty Nail”.

Rusty Nail is the mysterious and sadistic trucker who torments the main characters throughout the movie. Played by actor Matthew Kimbrough, Rusty Nail’s identity remains shrouded in mystery until the end.

The film’s tagline is “Don’t underestimate a stranger”.

The tagline of Joy Ride perfectly captures the theme of the movie – the danger of underestimating someone you don’t know. It serves as a warning to the characters and the audience about the perils that lie ahead.

The movie was largely shot in Utah.

The stunning landscapes of Utah serve as the backdrop for many scenes in Joy Ride. From vast deserts to winding mountain roads, the cinematography beautifully captures the isolation and vulnerability of the characters.

Joy Ride was inspired by classic road trip films.

The concept of Joy Ride was inspired by classic road trip movies like Duel (1971) and The Hitcher (1986). It takes the familiar trope of a journey turning into a nightmare and adds its own unique twists and turns.

The film’s budget was $23 million.

Joy Ride had a moderate budget of $23 million, which allowed the producers to create high-quality stunts, visual effects, and an immersive atmosphere. The investment paid off, as the film went on to be a box office success.
