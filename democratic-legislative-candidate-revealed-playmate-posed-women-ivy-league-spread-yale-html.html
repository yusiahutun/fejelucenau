Amy Biviano, the Democratic challenger in the 4th District House race, appeared in Playboy magazine in 1995

A Democrat who is vying for a legislative seat in the state House has come under fire after a picture of her posing for Playboy in a special 'Women of the Ivy League' edition was leaked today.

Amy Biviano, the democratic challenger in the 4th district house race to represent Spokane Valley has defended her decision to appear topless for the magazine in 1995 while she was a college junior at Yale University.

Her appearance in the magazine was revealed this afternoon on the website for the conservative Western Center for Journalism.

Defending her actions today, the married mother said that though it certainly isn't something she would do now, aged 37, it was an ego-boosting exercise when she was 20-years-old.

'It was one of the youthful college kinds of things. I was interested in pushing my limits. This was my small act of rebellion,' she said.

According to Seattle Weekly, she earned less than $500 for the shoot and when approached again by the magazine some years later, she refused.

At the time, she defended her decision saying: 'So do I believe that my future might be affected by posing for Playboy? Yes, I believe that it will.

'But, it has made a positive contribution to my life -- I gained a sense of self-reliance which I lacked before the posing scandal.

'It permanently changed me by making me think a little bit differently about myself – I’m now more of a risk-taker, fear social approval less, and know a bit more about what I’m capable of.

Amy's husband Andrew, right, said about the photograph: 'I'm the luckiest guy. How many guys do you know who married an Ivy League-educated Playboy model who also happens to be the most wonderful person in the world?'

Amy said today: 'I have gone on to build an extremely happy marriage of 15 years, have been blessed with amazing children, have been active in my church, and have worked and volunteered to help my community'

'I may never do something this controversial again, but it’s nice to know that I could and did.'

She said in a statement issued today: 'I learned a lot from my college experiences. Since then, I have gone on to build an extremely happy marriage of 15 years, have been blessed with amazing children, have been active in my church, and have worked and volunteered to help my community while living with a disability.'

Biviano is running against Republican state Rep Matt Shea who initially said the revelation was 'alarming'

Biviano is running against Republican state Rep Matt Shea who initially said the revelation was 'alarming' but later said this was not the kind of dirty tactic he wanted to drag into the race.

He issued a statement saying: 'I am saddened by the release by a national media outlet of my opponent's involvement in pornography. I strongly condemn the release of this information.

'This type of negative campaigning is exactly what is wrong with politics today. While these revelations are indeed alarming, my heart goes out to Amy and her family. My wife Viktoriya and I will continue to pray for her.'

The Democrat's husband Andrew Biviano, who was her boyfriend at the time she posed for the magazine, encouraged her to do the shoot, she said.

He said in a gushing statement to Seattle Weekly today: 'I'm the luckiest guy. How many guys do you know who married an Ivy League-educated Playboy model who also happens to be the most wonderful person in the world?'

Spokane County Republican Party Chairman Matthew Pederson said Biviano’s appearance in Playboy was well-known in political circles but that the party didn’t believe it was an issue.

'It’s something that we’ve known for a long time, but it’s not germane to the campaign,' Pederson said.
