Da Brat's biography

The actress was born on 14th April 1974 in Chicago, Illinois, in the United States. She was brought up in the West Side district of the city. Da Brat's parents are David Ray McCoy (father) and Beverly Calloway (mother). Her father is a businessman while her mother is a city bus driver.

Brat was raised in two different households since her parents were never married. She spent part of her childhood with her mother and grandmother, where she attended an apostolic church and sang in the choir. She has four half-siblings, namely Jehlan, Morgan, LisaRaye, and Cynthia.

The star joined Kenwood Academy during her sophomore and junior year. She ran track and played basketball while in school. In 1992, she graduated from the Academy of Scholastic Achievement, a continuation charter school that caters to at-risk students.

Music career

After her high school graduation, Harris participated in a local rap contest where she emerged as the winner. During the event, she met Kris Kross, who later introduced her to music producer Jermaine Dupri. Consequently, she was signed with the producer's recording label, So So Def.

She released her first album, Funkdafied, under the label. The album became an instant hit selling millions of album copies. A platinum certification followed this. She released her second studio album, Anuthatantrum, on 29th October 1996. Her second album earned Gold Certification and peaked at 5th place on the R&B Billboard.

Legal issues

The rapper has been on the wrong side of the law several times. In 2001 she pleaded guilty to misdemeanour reckless conduct after battering a woman with a gun over VIP seating in an Atlanta nightclub in 2000. She served a year's probation, performed 80 hours of community service and paid a $1,000 fine.

On 31st October 2007, she was involved in an altercation at a Halloween party where she attacked a hostess. She pleaded guilty to aggravated assault charges and was sentenced to three years in prison, seven years of probation, and 2000 hours of community service. The assault victim was awarded $6.4 million on 28th February 2014 to cover past and future injuries.

Sexuality
