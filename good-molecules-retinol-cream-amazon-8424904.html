This $8 Retinol Cream Gives Shoppers Brighter and Healthier-Looking Skin

Publish date: 2024-09-23

Retinol is one of those skincare ingredients that can feel intimidating. It’s known to have fantastic anti-aging benefits, but can also be harsh on the skin. So, if you’re not quite sure how to incorporate the powerhouse into your beauty routine, we may just have the answer. More than 9,000 Amazon shoppers have purchased the $8 Good Molecules Gentle Retinol Cream in the past month, per the retailer, and one confirmed it’s a “great formula for beginners.” 

The face cream is made with 0.1 percent retinol to smooth fine lines, plant-based bakuchiol to soothe and even out skin, grapeseed oil to lock in moisture, allantoin to calm inflammation, and acmella oleracea extract (a flowering plant) to reduce wrinkles. The formula is also vegan, cruelty free, and made without irritating fragrances. 

Depending on your skin’s sensitivity level, you can use this cream up to four nights a week, according to the brand. Simply massage a pea-size amount into clean skin after applying serum — just make sure to wear SPF during the day (regardless of the weather), as retinol can make your skin extra susceptible to sun-related burns and damage.

Shoppers can’t stop raving about the results they’re seeing from the Good Molecules cream. One reviewer said the formula “softened all [their] lines” and made their skin “look healthier” within a week of use, while another person said their “pores are smaller” and “skin is brighter and healthier-looking” after just two days. Someone else said their “skin looks younger, smoother, even-toned, and hydrated” within six weeks of using the product. 

Shoppers with mature skin are fans of the formula, too. A 61-year-old shopper said it “helps tighten pores,” adding that they feel “no tingling or irritation.” Plus, someone in their 50s said “people are complimenting” their previously dry, broken-out skin after incorporating the cream into their routine. Not to mention, another shopper deemed the moisturizer one of the “few products [they]... like almost instantly.”
