Jodie Byrne and Sam Alford get fairytale wedding in romantic lakeside ceremony

Publish date: 2024-08-21

They fell in love on reality TV show The Farmer Wants A Wife in 2012 – she was a travel agent from Sydney, he was a musterer and cattleman from Western Australia.

Now, Channel Nine stars Sam Alford and Jodie Byrne have had their happy ending, tying the knot in an idyllic lakeside ceremony in Nowra, New South Wales.

On a summer's day in January Jodie, 30, was a picture of bridal perfection stepping out in a stunning strapless cream wedding dress.

'It was such a magical day,' Jodie revealed of the summer-time wedding to Ok! Australia. 

Determined to create a relaxed and rustic country vibe, the pair settled on Nowra's The Driftwood as a venue, and the guests watched the lovebirds tie the knot on a flower-strewn lawn against a lakeside backdrop.

Al fresco: The natural surrounds were perfect for the couple who met in a farm setting on the Channel 9 show

Jumping for joy! A trio of bridesmaids donned floor length, pale peach gowns

A trio of bridesmaids donned floor length, pale peach gowns, as the head-over-heels pair said their vows in front of a beautifully styled canopy of vines and flowers that hung from a vintage door frame.

While the groomsmen looked handsome in their attire, clapping are the loved-up pair sealed their union with a kiss.

A guitarist played sheet music while the guests mingled in the relaxed atmosphere with Jodie revealing the day was everything they'd dreamed of.

'We wanted it to be a great garden party and it was everything we could have imagined and more,' she said. 

Idyllic setting: Travel agent Jodie was joined by a pair of gorgeous flower girls as they sat under the shade of vintage umbrellas 

Swing when you're winning: Sam proposed at the Daintree Rainforest in December 2012

While her husband said of the day, 'This is our happily ever after.'

After enjoying the beautiful outdoor setting guests headed indoors to the rustic barn for the all important cutting of the cake.

A beautiful tiered cream cake adorned with blooms was just one of the many carefully thought out touches of the day.

Vintages umbrellas for the flower girls, hand drawn signs on wooden boards and glass bottles holding simple flowers, all added to the homemade vibe of the day.

A romantic at heart Sam proposed to Jodie on a piece of driftwood during a Christmas getaway to the Daintree Rainforest in December 2012.

'We sat down on his giant piece of driftwood ... he looked a bit nervous,' Jodie told Yahoo 7 last year.

'I had no idea, I think until he started talking about me being his wife one day. And I was like "are you proposing" and he was like "maybe I am" and then he pulled out the ring and it was real.'
