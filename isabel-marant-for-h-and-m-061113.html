Surprise: Isabel Marant For H&amp;M

Publish date: 2024-03-21

Media Platforms Design Team

We've become accustomed to the designer collaboration cycle, but every once and a while one comes along that can surprise even the most weary fashion soul. Welcome Isabel Marant for H&M. The notoriously press-shy, e-commerce reticent, cool girl go-to is taking Paris to Stockholm for the collection, launching November 14. Marant is slated to design a collection of must-haves, a wardrobe inspired by her signature rockstar-boho fusion for the French girl style — and for the first time, a line for men.

"I am flattered by this collaboration," Marant explains, "H&M works with the best designers... I aim to creating something real, that women want to wear in their everyday lives, with a certain carelessness, which I think is very Parisian: you dress up, but do not pay too much attention and still look sexy." The collection of ready-to-wear and accessories will hit 250 doors, presumably ones with lines stretched before them.

Kerry Pieri is Harper's Bazaar's Digital Fashion/Features Director. Kerry oversees the fashion vertical for BAZAAR.com, including launching digital covers, styling celebrity features, and managing recurring features including Brand Watch and My Life in Three Looks. In addition, Kerry edits the “best of” each season, from shoes to bags, denim and coats, and is consistently looking for shifts in the fashion realm to lend a voice. Kerry formerly served as the Editorial Director of  StyleCaster.com and as a producer at Full Frontal Fashion. She lives in New York with her husband Steve, and baby girl Lila Sky.
