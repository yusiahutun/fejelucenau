Houseplant of the week: flaming katy

Publish date: 2024-10-06
Houseplant of the weekLife and style

This low-maintenance charmer blooms in shades of red, pink, orange and yellow for weeks at a time

Why will I love it?
Kalanchoe blossfeldiana is a cheap and cheerful houseplant adored for its vibrant colours and minimal care needs. Its stunning blooms in shades of red, pink, orange, yellow and white can last up to eight weeks in spring/summer, and it’s widely available – you can usually find one at the supermarket.

Light or shade?
Bright, indirect light – although it can tolerate some direct sunlight for short spurts.

Where should I put it?
Flaming katy thrives in a bright spot, making it an excellent addition to a sunny windowsill or a well-lit corner.

How do I keep it alive?
Allow the soil to dry out slightly between waterings; overwatering can lead to root rot. Use a potting mix that drains well to prevent this. Feed the plant with a balanced houseplant fertiliser monthly during the growing season. Keep it warm; it appreciates temperatures between 15C and 24C.

Did you know …
These plants made their way to Europe from Madagascar in 1932, courtesy of German botanist Robert Blossfield, after whom this variety, blossfeldiana, is named. With their impressive and enduring blooming period, flaming katy plants make wonderful gifts, symbolising enduring affection, lasting beauty and resilience.
