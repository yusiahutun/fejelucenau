FAQs

1. What is the history of Green Lane Masjid?

Green Lane Masjid was established in 1979 and has grown over the years to become one of the largest mosques in the UK. It has played a crucial role in serving the local Muslim community and promoting interfaith dialogue.

2. Are non-Muslims allowed to visit Green Lane Masjid?

Yes, Green Lane Masjid welcomes visitors of all faiths to explore its facilities and learn about Islamic culture. The mosque regularly hosts open days and community events to promote understanding and foster positive relationships.

3. What facilities does Green Lane Masjid offer?

Green Lane Masjid provides a range of facilities, including prayer halls, classrooms, a library, a community center, and even a gymnasium. These amenities cater to the needs of the local community and promote a holistic approach to worship and personal development.

4. Does Green Lane Masjid offer educational programs?

Yes, Green Lane Masjid is committed to providing educational opportunities for both children and adults. The mosque offers Islamic classes, Tajweed (Quranic recitation) lessons, and various educational programs to enrich the knowledge and spiritual understanding of its attendees.

5. How does Green Lane Masjid contribute to the community?

Green Lane Masjid actively engages in community outreach programs, supporting the homeless, providing food banks, and organizing charitable events. The mosque also collaborates with local organizations to promote social cohesion and address key issues affecting the community.

6. Can I attend religious events at Green Lane Masjid?

Absolutely! Green Lane Masjid hosts a variety of religious events throughout the year, including Eid celebrations, Ramadan activities, and Islamic lectures. These events offer a chance to learn more about Islamic traditions and engage in meaningful spiritual experiences.

7. Is there parking available at Green Lane Masjid?

Yes, Green Lane Masjid has parking facilities available for visitors. However, it is recommended to check in advance, as parking may be limited during peak times or events.
