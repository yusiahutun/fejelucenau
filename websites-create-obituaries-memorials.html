7 Websites to Create Obituaries and Memorials Online

Publish date: 2024-04-05

Losing a loved one is never an easy thing to deal with, and trying to find meaningful ways to remember them can be difficult. Obituaries and memorials are a great way to highlight your loved ones' experiences through the lives that they lived, But when you are mourning, it can be difficult to think about how to structure an obituary and what to include in it.

Fortunately, there are tools available online to guide you during that process. In this article, you’ll find seven websites to help you create obituaries and memorials for your loved ones.

1. Obituare

If you want to create a long-lasting memory of your loved one, this website helps you do that. Obituare is a website that allows you to create an obituary for your loved ones, and guides you through every step.

Instead of being restricted by space and budget limitations for having a newspaper obituary, you can opt for a once-off lifetime payment of $99 on Obituare.

This means that you gain access to multiple features, which include, an unlimited number of words, a donation link where people can donate funds to assist the family of the deceased, as well as a photo feature on Obituare’s home page. The platform also offers a free plan with three day access, and a premium 30-day access plan that goes for $29.

2. Remembered.com

Remembered.com is a platform centered around remembering your loved one through media content. On this website, you can create an online memorial website where you can tell your loved one’s story.

The website is free to use and has an easy interface with unique features, such as a memorial slideshow, and a tribute feature where people can visit the memorial and light a candle.

If you're not sure how to start creating a memorial for your loved one, you can seek inspiration from memorials that other people have created on the website.

If you’re looking for ways to help your loved ones cope with grief, you can create a memorial wall to invite them to share their stories and memories.

3. Ever Loved

Ever Loved helps you remember your loved ones by providing free tools for you to create a memorial website in their honor. The memorial website is community-driven; that way, friends and family members of the deceased can add their memories and condolences on the website.

One of the special features on this website is the ability to create a timeline that highlights the years where important moments took place in your loved one’s life. These could be the year they were born, the year they graduated, when they got married, and anything else that might have been important.

Similar to the timeline, Ever Loved also helps you create an obituary, where you can add details of their life achievements and the loved ones they’ve left behind.

4. Elegant Memorials

Elegant Memorials provides you with a convenient way to create a memorial for your loved ones who have deceased. You can choose from a wide range of design ideas and templates that are easily customizable for your funeral program, memorial card, or obituary.

Most templates on the site cost $24.99. The website also provides instructional guides and samples on how to write a eulogy and an obituary.

When you are grieving a loved one, and you are still coming to terms with losing them, you might not be keen on engaging in certain tasks, like making a funeral program. Fortunately, Elegant Memorials offers a service where they edit templates on your behalf. All you have to do is purchase a template and call them with all the necessary details.

Elegant Memorials doesn’t only focus on helping you create a funeral program or an obituary, you can also access information on how to plan a funeral or memorial service.

5. Quick Funeral

Quick Funeral is a website that provides you with a variety of free tools to ease the process of arranging a funeral. On this website, you’ll access tools to help you create professional funeral programs to commemorate your loved one in the best way.

Poems on a funeral card represent a special way to celebrate your loved one, and allows you to give them the sentimental farewell they deserve. One of the unique features found on this website is a range of poems and prayers you can choose from to add on a funeral card.

Other templates you can find on this website are: obituaries, bi fold templates, funeral bookmarks, and how-to guides on how to create these funeral essentials.

6. Keeper

Keeper is a memorial website that aims to help users keep the memory of their deceased loved ones alive. This is another collaborative memorial platform that allows you to invite close friends and family members to share stories or to merely engage on the platform as a way to honor your loved one.

On Keeper, you’ll get to upload images and videos, create a biography and obituary, host a guestbook for tributes, and map out a family tree. That’s not all, Keeper also gives you the chance to host a virtual memorial. That way, you can accommodate people who live far away and have busy lives, and give them the opportunity to celebrate the life of the person who’s passed away.

Keeper has a free and paid version with unlimited features and goes for a once off payment of $74.

7. Lastly.com

If you’ve always wanted to write down your life story but didn’t know where to start, this website is for you. Lastly.com allows you to preserve your own life story to leave behind when you depart from this world.

To start the process, Lastly.com will guide you by providing prompts which you’ll answer to help build up your life story. As you review the moments of your life using these prompts, it won’t feel as stressful as trying to draft your life story from scratch.

If you’re not planning on drafting your life story, you can still use Lastly.com to preserve the memory of someone close to you. The website offers a seven-day free trial, and costs $45 per year.

Give Your Loved Ones a Lasting Memory

The internet helps us move through life in many interesting ways, and one of those ways is by helping you honor your loved ones. The above-mentioned websites will make remembering the deceased less of a burden. Whether you need an obituary, a memorial site, or a funeral program, there’s something for you.

Death isn’t an easy thing to deal with, especially when you’ve lost someone close to you. Which is why it’s important to acknowledge how it affects you, and take the necessary steps to heal and deal with the loss.
