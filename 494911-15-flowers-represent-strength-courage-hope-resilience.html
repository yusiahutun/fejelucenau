15 flowers that represent strength, courage, hope, and resilience

Publish date: 2024-03-10

Everyone wants to remain strong and courageous enough to overcome different challenges in life. Unfortunately, this is not always the case, as life is full of ups and downs. Connecting with flowers that represent strength will give you inner power, courage, hope, and resilience.

Flowers that represent strength are an everyday inspiration. They serve as a reminder of how strong and courageous one can be. Many people use flowers as a form of non-verbal communication when words can’t be enough to express themselves.

Flowers that represent strength

Discovering the diversity and beauty of flowers is a journey of intrigue and marvel. What flower symbolizes strength? The following are 15 flowers representing strength, courage, hope, and resilience.

1. Allium

An allium is an ornamental but edible flower from the onion family. It originates from the northern hemisphere and comes in different colours. These colours include yellow, white, purple, pink, and blue. Generically, allium means strength, patience, unity, and prosperity.

These flowers are an excellent gift for positive reinforcement to anyone going through challenging times. Since they grow from one bulb, they represent the strength of unity. This makes them a great option for couples, family events or friendships.

2. Potentilla

This is a hardy flower primarily found in the northern hemisphere. The plant endures tough environments such as heat, drought, dry weather, and salty air. However, the flower is a great survivor since it needs little care. Potentilla comes in yellow, white, red, and pink.

The flower is associated with the following:

  • Endurance
  • Inner strength
  • Health
  • Power
  • Wellbeing
  • Loyalty
  • Maternal love
  • Womanhood

3. Valeriana

Valeriana is one of the flowers that symbolize strength and courage. The flower has more than 200 species primarily grown for medicinal purposes. It is useful in minimizing the effects of insomnia, stress, anxiety, epilepsy, headaches, and muscle pain.

The flower grows in different colours, such as white, pink, and lavender. It represents mental and physical health in getting over health challenges. Therefore, it’s one of the best choices for people seeking encouragement to overcome a health issue.

4. Gladiolus

Gladiolus flowers have over 300 species and do well in native sub-Saharan Africa. Its name is of Latin origin, gladius, meaning sword. In the past, Roman fighters wore the flower around their neck to gain strength for battle.

The flower represents:

  • Courage
  • Character strength
  • Moral integrity
  • Endurance
  • Dignity

It is an excellent gift for anyone undergoing different challenges, such as divorce or sickness.

5. Phacelia

Phacelia, also known as lacy phacelia (beauty) or scorpionweed (curly-shaped flower clusters), grows in north and south America. They grow in tough soils and tolerate harsh weather conditions like hot weather and drought.

They come in either blue or purple blooms. Phacelia flowers are associated with the following:

  • Determination
  • Strength

6. Cistus

Cistus are one of the plants that represent strength. They do well in the Mediterranean regions resisting heat and other dry conditions. Since it also grows in dry and rocky places, it is called the rock rose.

The flower is a symbol of:

  • Endurance
  • Strength
  • Ability to be yourself in any environment

You can choose to gift the flowers to another person as a form of inspiration or grow it in your garden.

7. Echinacea

Echinacea flowers symbolize strength and healing. They are among the most consumed plants thanks to their values. Indians, Europeans, and Native Americans use it for medical purposes. Thanks to its medicinal use, the flower represents strength and good health.

Many people use the flowers as a remedy for the following illnesses:

  • Insect bites
  • Colds
  • Sore throats
  • Toothaches
  • Stomach cramps
  • Wounds

8. Astrantia

Astrantia flowers are native to Asia and Europe. Its moniker is derived from the Latin word Aster which means star. Therefore, it is also referred to as masterwort or star herb. The plant does amazingly well in forests, humid mountains, and shady places. They come in purple, red, pink, and white.

Astrantia represents the following:

  • Protection
  • Strength
  • Endurance
  • Womanhood
  • Elegance
  • Innocence
  • Grace
  • Youth

If you want someone to awaken their inner passion, courage, and strength, Astrantia flowers are a good gift option.

9. Antirrhinum

Antirrhinum is a flower that symbolises strength because it can thrive in dry and rocky grounds. It represents the ability of an individual to go past challenging moments and become the best versions of themselves. If you need a little motivation, this is the right choice of flowers to go for.

Despite growing in harsh conditions, they emerge vibrant and tall. This unique quality makes the flower an excellent symbol of admiration and respect. A bouquet of Antirrhinum is a good gift for your parents or mentor.

10. Aquilegia

Aquilegia does well in the Northern Hemisphere. The name originates from the Latin word Aquilla meaning eagle. The flower carries this name because its five spurs resemble an eagle's claws.

Since it grows well in meadows, high mountains, grasslands, and woodlands, many people associate it with inherent inspiration and inner strength to achieve anything. Therefore, you can gift it to someone who needs the awakening to tap into their inner strength.

11. Cactus flower

Cactus flowers thrive in harsh weather conditions. Many people keep them as indoor plants since they can go for days without water making them low maintenance. Their blooms symbolize resilience and strength.

Cactus flowers come in different colours, each with a different meaning. For instance, orange and pink symbolize youth, while yellow and white symbolize strength and endurance.

12. Amsonia

Amsonias, also also known as blue stars, are flowers that signify strength. They are native to North America. The flowers thrive with the right conditions making them an excellent choice to plant in your garden. Amsonia flowers are associated with physical and mental strength.

13. Epimedium

Epimedium is native to European and Asian regions such as Eastern Asia and the Mediterranean Sea. It is also known as the Bishop's Hat. It is an excellent addition to one's garden because of its unique heart shape.

It represents strength and fertility. Since it does well in dry soil, during drought seasons and in dry places, it symbolizes endurance and the ability to overcome challenging things and moments in one's life. It shows one’s ability to grow and blossom in hardships.

14. Daffodil

This is a bright yellow and orange flower that symbolizes strength. Different parts of the plants are believed to carry different meanings. For instance, yellow represents hope, happiness, and good luck.

The tall stem represents determination, ambition, and courage. You can gift these flowers to someone looking for the courage and strength of their rebirth or starting fresh.

15. Nasturtium

Nasturtium is one of the flowers that stand for strength and hardiness. This makes them one of the perfect choices to symbolize fortitude and courage. They do well in harsh conditions and are seen as a symbol of hope and resilience.
