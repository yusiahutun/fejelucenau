What happened to Big Pun?

Big Pun, one of the best rappers of all time, was about 400 pounds when the record came out, but as he gained popularity, he added weight. He attempted to lose weight at the advice of his friend rapper Fat Joe, even enrolling in the Duke University diet program in North Carolina in 1999.

Although not permanently, Big Pun lost weight. He gained the 80 pounds he had lost and continued to do so. Because of his bulk, even going around and taking care of daily tasks became difficult.

He was in high demand because of his incredible abilities and appeared with Fat Joe in a cameo on Jennifer Lopez's song "Feelin' So Good." On February 5, 2000, Big Pun was scheduled to perform the song on Saturday Night Live with Lopez and Fat Joe, but he cancelled due to illness.

Big Pun's death and legacy

Due to Big Pun's weight, he had health issues. He had trouble breathing on February 7 and passed out in his hotel room. The paramedics attempted to revive him without success. The rapper was only 28 years old and weighed approximately 700 pounds when he passed away from heart failure.

Puerto Ricans and hip-hop communities lamented the loss of one of their stars. A few days after his passing, thousands of followers showed up at his funeral in the Bronx.

Several well-known friends also shared their sorrow over his passing. Lopez, who spoke to MTV, said, "He was a source of pride for the Latin community, a brilliant artist, and a terrific person." Fat Joe admitted to The New York Times, saying, "I lost a sibling."

Big Pun's net worth

At the time of his passing, Big Pun had a net worth of $3 million. One of the greatest MCs of all time, Big Pun first appeared in the underground hip-hop scene of New York City in the 1990s.
