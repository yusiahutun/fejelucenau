4. Emilio Delgado - March 2022

He received a call to test out for a new kids' TV show while hunting for acting work in Los Angeles. Delgado was selected for the job and held the record for the longest-running role for a Mexican American actor on television while portraying "Luis" on "Sesame Street" for decades.

Delgado joined the cast in 1971, remained a regular until 2016, and participated in the 2019 50th anniversary celebration of the program. He died of blood cancer.

5. Lauro Cavazos - March 2022

He was the first Latino member of the U.S. Cabinet. Cavazos started his schooling in a two-room schoolhouse on the King Ranch in Texas, where his father was the foreman. Even though there was segregation in South Texas schools in the 1930s, Cavazos' father campaigned to get his kid into a white school in nearby Kingsville. No cause of death was given.

Carmen was raised in an educated household in Cuba. She attended art school in Paris and spent many years honing her painting technique in New York City. 8. Carmen Salinas - December 2021

The famous Mexican politician, actress, and theatrical producer Carmen Salinas passed away in December 2021 at 82, over a month after a stroke that put her in a coma. Her family reported her passing in Cuauhtémoc, Mexico City, in a tweet honouring her enormous influence over six decades in the Mexican entertainment sector.

9. Raymundo Capetillo - July 2020

According to a tweet from the Mexican government's "Extensión Cultural INBAL" account, actor Raymundo Capetillo passed away at 76. The actor's niece Tanya Roberta made claims to the media claiming that Capetillo had COVID-19 issues and was hospitalized in Mexico.

10. Vicente Fernandez- December 2021

Vicente is on the list of dead Hispanic singers. According to his family, Vicente Fernández passed away in December at a hospital in Guadalajara at 81 years old. Vicente was a towering giant of Mexican ranchera music whose strong voice shaped the lives of generations of followers throughout Latin America.
