Sofia Coppola and Thomas Mars both cut very casual figures while walking their dog in New York City

Publish date: 2024-09-11

Sofia Coppola and her husband, Thomas Mars, were spotted walking their dog in New York City on Wednesday afternoon.

The 50-year-old director and her 44-year-old spouse appeared to be making the most of the pleasant springtime weather as they took a leisurely stroll along the Hudson River Greenway.

The couple has been married ever since 2011 and share two children.

Couple's day out: Sofia Coppola and her husband, Thomas Mars, were spotted walking their dog in New York City on Thursday afternoon

Coppola was dressed in a white graphic-printed t-shirt and kept a light pink sweater wrapped around her waist.

She contrasted her top choices with a pair of Nike sweat pants and a set of multicolored high-top sneakers/

The Marie Antoinette director's brunette hair fell onto her shoulders and the nape of her neck during her dog-walking trip. She appeared to be makeup-free.

Mars cut a similarly casual figure in a light blue button-up shirt, a pair of slim-fitting jeans and a set of high-top brown boots.

Taking it easy: The filmmaker and the singer both cut very casual figures while making their way along the Hudson River Greenway

Coppola and Mars first met in 1999 when the Phoenix frontman contributed to the soundtrack of her second film, The Virgin Suicides.

That same year, the director became married to fellow filmmaker Spike Jonze, with whom she was first romantically connected in 1992.

The former couple would go on to split up five years after they initially tied the knot, and she would use aspects of her former partner's personality for a character in Lost In Translation. 

The director went on to become linked to Mars at a certain point after the dissolution of her first marriage, and they went on to make their union official in 2011.

The two later welcomed two daughters named Romy and Cosima, aged 14 and 10, respectively. 

Going strong: Coppola married the Phoenix frontman in 2011, after having divorced fellow filmmaker Spike Jonze in 2003; she is pictured with her daughter Romy in 2019

Proud parents: The couple also shares a younger daughter named Cosima, who was born in 2010; the family is pictured in 2012

Coppola spoke at length about the balance between her role as a parent and her position as a filmmaker during an interview with The Independent, where she noted that becoming a mother changed how she structured her life.

She expressed, 'You start to realize how you have to be more organized with your time. And it's kind of the opposite of an artist, [where] you need to just meander...For anyone creative, it's a definite adjustment.'

The director then elaborated on how her most recent film, On The Rocks, dealt with the compromises that she has made as a mother and the line between her work and her marriage. 

Staying busy; The filmmaker is set to direct a limited series adaptation of the 1913 novel The Custom Of The Country for Apple TV+; she is seen on the set of The Beguiling in 2017

The filmmaker specifically noted that she was trying to capture a period of time with her feature, noting that, 'I'm just trying to take where I was at that moment in my life where my kids were younger.' 

Coppola is currently set to direct an adaptation of Edith Wharton's 1913 novel The Custom Of The Country.

The forthcoming limited series will follow a girl from the American Midwest who attempts to raise her profile in New York's high society.

The finer details of the project, including casting and a release date, are currently being kept from the public, and it is unknown if the filmmaker will attempt to modernize the story.

Like her last directorial effort, On The Rocks, The Custom Of The Country will be made available on the Apple TV+ streaming service.
