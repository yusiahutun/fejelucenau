The Origins of Crystal Palace

Crystal Palace Football Club was founded in 1905 and originated from the Crystal Palace Exhibition building, which was previously situated in Hyde Park for the Great Exhibition of 1851.

Promotion to the Premier League

In the 2012-2013 season, Crystal Palace achieved promotion to the English Premier League by winning the Championship Play-Off Final against Watford at Wembley Stadium.

Iconic Stadium

The Crystal Palace team plays their home matches at Selhurst Park, a historic stadium located in the London Borough of Croydon. The stadium has a capacity of over 26,000 spectators.

Rivalries

Crystal Palace has fierce rivalries with other London clubs including Brighton & Hove Albion and Millwall. Matches between these teams are highly anticipated and often result in intense and passionate clashes.

Record Goalscorer

As of now, Peter Simpson holds the record for the most goals scored in a single season for Crystal Palace. He netted an impressive 46 goals during the 1929-1930 season.

FA Cup Finalists

In 1990, Crystal Palace reached the FA Cup Final for the first time in their history. Although they ultimately lost to Manchester United in a thrilling replay, it was a significant achievement for the club.

The “Five Year Plan”

During the 2010s, Crystal Palace adopted the so-called “Five Year Plan,” aiming to establish the club as a consistent competitor in the Premier League. The plan laid the foundation for the team’s recent success and stability.

Club Crest

The iconic Crystal Palace emblem features a shield with a central eagle, symbolizing strength and pride. The club’s traditional colors of red and blue are also prominently displayed in the crest.
