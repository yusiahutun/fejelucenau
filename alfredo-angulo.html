Alfredo Angulo | Professional Career 

After his amateur career, Alfredo was already enrolled in the professional sites of Arizona. During the tenure, he fought in the Light Middleweight divisions at first.

By the tenure of 2008, he had already maintained 13 victories, among which ten were just by knockout. 

Alfredo Angulo vs. Andrey Tsurkan 

On October 4, 2008, Alfredo Angulo faced Andrey Tsurkan in Pechanga Resort & Casino, Temecula, California, USA. The bout was aired on HBO Boxing After Dark. 

During the match, the two commenced right through a series of punches at a high pace.

Initially, Andrey had taken the lead; however, Alfredo closely followed, but in the end, Alfredo gained a nasty cut above his right eye. 

However, this only kept Angulo going deeper into the fight as he averaged a hundred punches in every single round.

Altogether, at the end of the eleventh round, Andrey’s manager waived a white towel to stop the fight, and Alfredo took the win. 

Alfredo Angulo vs. Harry Joe Yorgey 

After Andrey, Alfredo had collected a win from former NABO light Welterweight champion Cosme Rivera. In brief, he won through knockout on February 14, 2009. 

Right after it, Angulo fought against an undefeated Harry Joe Yorgey at the XL Center.

To illustrate, the bout took place as the co-feature on the card headlined by the light heavyweight rematch between Chad Dawson and Glen Johnson. 

At first, Harry took the lead in the initial round. However, Alfredo took the initiative in the other two matches. Altogether, he bagged the win on the third round via knockout.

According to CompuBox, Alfredo had a total of 108 punches in the fight. 

Anyways, with the victory, Alfredo took the interim WBO light-middleweight title.

“I never worked this hard for anything in my life. All the hard work paid off. I knew I would be victorious.”
-Alfredo Angulo 

Afterward, on April 24, 2010, Alfredo had his foremost title defense over Joel Julio. With the 11th round victory from the bout, Alfredo retained the WBO interim 154-pound title.

In the meantime, it was also his third straight victory via knockout. 

Alfredo Angulo vs. Joachim “Ti-Joa” Alcine

By the time of Angulo’s fight with Alcine, he had already established his name as an exciting boxer.

In addition to that, they featured for the fight on July 17, 2010, on the undercard of the Timothy Bradley vs. Luis Carlos Abregu Welterweight battle.

By the end of the fight, Alfredo took his victory over former WBA Super Welterweight Champion Joachim “Ti-Joa” Alcine. At the same time, he gained the WBC Continental Americas Light Middleweight Title. 

Following Fights

After the title, Alfredo faced Joe Gomez in El Nido Sports Center, Mexicali, Mexico. Therewith, he defended his title (by knockout) as their fight was held on August 20, 2011.

However, he lost the title in his next fight over James Kirkland (by knockout) in Centro de Cancún, Cancún, Mexico. 

After a loss, Alfredo jumped back to two wins in a row over Raul Casarez (by knockout) and Jorge Silva (by unanimous decision).

On June 8, 2013, Alfredo lost the match by knockout over Erislandy Lara in Home Depot Center, Carson, California, U.S.

Injury from Erislandy Lara

During the bout against Erislandy Lara, Alfredo gained a gruesome eye injury that ended the fight right away. As Alfredo’s face suffered a complete wreck, they expected him to have fractured his left eye’s orbital bone.

As all states it to be a clean punch from Lara, Alfredo rephrases it to be a dirty job. According to him, Lara had thumbed him twice in the eye.

Anyways, right after the match ended, they took Alfredo to Harbor-UCLA Medical Center. 

However, they didn’t have any MRI or X-ray for the injury and stated that Alfredo had no fractures. Indeed, many were disappointed that no one checked out his eye for him during the match. 

“The ref (Raul Caiz. Sr.) stopped it without checking the situation or giving a count. A regular punch would not do that (damage). It’s dirty fighting by Lara, again. But I won the love and respect of nonbelievers, too.”

Following the fight with Lara, he had another two losses over Canelo Álvarez (knockout) James de la Rosa (unanimous decision). In 2015, Alfredo took two wins against Delray Raines and Hector Munoz. 

However, after the year and later on, he was followed by yet another two losses. 

Alfredo Angulo vs. Peter Quillin

On September 21, 2019, Alfredo Angulo faced Peter Quillin in the main event of a Premier Boxing Champions card. Their match was held at Rabobank Arena in Bakersfield, California; they depicted the fight as the best match of 2019. 

On an important note, Alfredo dominated the fight as Quillin showcased a slow pace, while Alfredo had more aggressive and heavier hitting. All in all, Alfredo took the victory via a split decision. 

Alfredo Angulo vs. Vladimir Hernandez

As his last fight until now, Alfredo Angulo faced the late replacement of the match, Vladimir Hernandez, on August 29, 2020. The bout took place at Microsoft Theater, Los Angeles, California, as the co-feature event.

To illustrate, the match commenced with Hernandez dominating Alfredo with his punches. During the tenure, Alfredo got slow, and as the game lasted for ten rounds, Hernandez took the victory. 

Most important, the two broke the CompuBox record for a super middleweight bout with their 2,137 punches. Altogether, Hernandez had claimed the victory via 10 round decision by the scores 98-92, 98-92, and 98-92.

Indeed, the fight bought an unexpected turn for Alfredo, and he is on handling decisions about his further move.

Alfredo Angulo | Visa Issues

Back in 2010, Alfredo Angulo had legal visa issues problems that kept him in the detention center. Of course, a short beak for athletes is a big deal; however, it gets a big talk when such breaks end up in detention facilities. 

Likewise, during that time, Alfredo spent seven months in an Immigration and Naturalization Service detention facility in El Centro, Calif.

According to the sources, the boxer had overstayed his U.S. visa and had been illegally in the country. 

Having said this, Alfredo is not a drug dealer, molester, or any person with criminal records. He just had an expired visa, and with it, he could either stay illegally or go through the legal process. 

As he went through the legal process, the work elongated, and he ended up in custody for more than half a year.

Fortunately, his manager handled all the work professionally, and he was out after a long time. 

“I’m here legally. Everything is fine. I had no idea when I would get out, and I kept thinking about my daughter. Now, I’m back in my town, and it feels great. I always knew that one day I would be free to fight again; the question was always when.”
-Alfredo Angulo
