12 most famous landmarks in Kenya that you need to visit

Publish date: 2024-02-02

Landmarks give a sense of a place. These monuments and natural wonders connect the world to a destination and inspire travel. Kenya is home to some of the most amazing landmarks in the world that date back to the ancient period. Some of these landmarks are natural, while others are man-made. So, which are the most famous landmarks in Kenya that you need to visit in 2022?

Kenya is one of the most popular countries in Africa. It is renowned for its natural landscapes, an abundance of mountains, national parks, and other iconic sites. These recognizable natural and artificial sites have long been used as useful tools for navigation. Without these landmarks, Kenya would not be too interesting to visit.

List of the most famous landmarks in Kenya

From ancient ruins to opulent buildings, there are many iconic landmarks located all around the country that make Kenya a great destination to visit. According to Demand Africa, the following are the most famous landmarks in the country.

1. Mount Kenya

This is the most famous landmark in Kenya. Mount Kenya is the second tallest mountain in Africa and the tallest mountain in Kenya. Its elevation range is approximately 2200 m to 5200 m. The twin summit peaks of Batian and Nelion are 5199 m and 5188 m, respectively. Lenana peak is 4985 m above sea level.

UNESCO inscribed it as one of the most impressive landscapes in the region. The jagged summit peaks, sharp cliffs, and snowcapped crags can be seen from a distance when the mountain is not shrouded in clouds. Climbing Mt Kenya is on many people's bucket lists when visiting the country.

2. Menengai Crater

This is one of the most intriguing destinations in Nakuru County. The Menengai Crater is an extinct or dormant volcano that sits on the rift valley floor, covering it with caldera lava flows. It is a large caldera believed to have formed by volcanic activity millions of years ago. The crater is about 2500m above sea level and has a diameter of 12km.

Visitors are drawn to the plunging cliffs, massive circular rim, and the rugged terrain on the caldera's floor. It is surrounded by lush forest and is protected by the Kenya Forest Service. Animals like the mountain reedbuck, mongoose, vervet monkeys, and many others call Menengai crater home.

3. Mau Mau Caves

This is one of the historical sites in Kenya. Mau Mau Caves is located on the foothills of Mt Kenya near Nanyuki. These caves have withstood the test of time and serve as a solemn reminder of the British Colonial Empire's bloodiest war. The epic caves are renowned for their historical significance.

They used to be storage and hideout zones for the Mau Mau freedom fighters during the pre-colonial period. These caves have a variety of other attractions, including the birdlife and indigenous forest full of the Columbus monkey.

4. Old Fort Jesus

Old Fort Jesus is one of Kenya's most famous man-made landmarks. It is located in Old Town Mombasa. The Fort is accredited as a UNESCO world heritage centre and is one of the most brilliant structures from the 16th century. The Portuguese built Fort Jesus in 1593-1596.

At that time of construction, Mombasa was a transit place for trade and a gateway to India. The Fort was positioned to guard and protect the Old Port of Mombasa from invasion. It is one of the finest examples of 16th-century Portuguese military architecture. The property covers an area of 2.36 hectares.

5. Lake Victoria

This is the largest lake in Africa and the biggest tropical lake in the world. It lies mainly in Tanzania and Uganda but borders Kenya. The lake was formed approximately 400,000 years old. It covers an area of 26,828 square miles or 68,800 square km.

The lake's surface is 1,134 m or 3720 feet above sea level. Lake Victoria is home to more than 200 species of fish, amphibians, reptiles, birds and animals. It contains approximately 1,000 islands, ranging from tiny spits of land to larger inhabited islets.

6. Bomas of Kenya

Bomas of Kenya is one of the most famous landmarks in Nairobi. It is a tourist village located in Langata, Nairobi, approximately 10km from the CBD. Bomas of Kenya first opened their doors in 1971. It was established to preserve, maintain and promote the rich and diverse cultural values of Kenyan ethnic groups.

Bomas of Kenya features replicas of traditional homesteads from 23 ethnic groups. It is also home to the largest auditorium in Africa, where traditional dances are performed. This location is one of the best places to visit if you want to learn more about the lifestyle, music, art and culture of Kenyan tribes.

7. Jomo Kenyatta Mausoleum

The mausoleum is located at Parliament Buildings in Nairobi. It is one of the few architectural masterpieces that are rarely open to the public. The place is heavily guarded by the Kenya Defense Forces soldiers.

This is where the body of the first president of Kenya, Mzee Jomo Kenyatta, lies. He died on August 22, 1978, of a heart attack. The mausoleum mostly attracts heads of state visiting the country and top dignitaries who wish to pay homage to the former president.

8. State House

This is the most significant place in Kenya because it is the official residence of the president of Kenya. It was built in 1907 during the colonial era and was previously referred to as Government House in Kenya. State House is located along State House Road, off Kenyatta Avenue, a few minutes' drive from the CBD.

Mzee Jomo Kenyatta, the first president of Kenya, was the first native occupant of the State House. It is the residence of the current president Uhuru Kenyatta. State House mainly hosts visiting heads of state from other countries, but there are special invites for dinners and luncheons.

9. Mnarani Ruins

Mnarani Ruins is located in Kilifi District, Coast province, along the Mombasa- Malindi road. They are the remains of two mosques. The history of these Mnarani Ruins dates back to the 13th century, when Omani Arabs came to the East African Coast for trade. Mnarani was destroyed by the Galla in the early 17th century during the Wagalla Raid.

The ruins were gazetted in March 1929. Some of the favourite attractions on the site include the thick baobab trees and tombs. This site also features a Museum and a snake park where you can view several snake species.

10. Gede Ruins

This famous historical and archaeological site is located in Gede Town, approximately 120 km from Mombasa town. Gede ruins are the remains of a Swahili town whose origin can be traced back to the 12th century. It was gazetted as a National Monument in 1927.

The site was excavated between 1948 and 1958, and many buildings and artefacts were uncovered. Today the monument is managed by the National Museums of Kenya. The beautiful indigenous forests, tamarind, and baobabs are major tourist attractions. At the entrance to the Gedi Ruins is the Kipepo Butterfly Project.

11. Kenyatta International Convention Centre

This is a 28-story building located in Nairobi, Kenya, and it is one of the tallest buildings in the country. Its construction was commissioned by the first president of the Republic of Kenya, Mzee Jomo Kenyatta, in 1967 and was completed by 1973.

KICC has the capacity to hold both large and small events, both indoors and outdoors. It boasts of being the most prominent facility for meetings in East Africa and has hosted quite a number of regional and international conferences. The main attractions of this landmark include the helipad and rooftop lookout.

12. Tsavo National Park

Tsavo is one of the oldest national parks in the country. The park is located near Voi Town in Taita Taveta County. It comprises two separate parks, Tsavo East National Park and Tsavo West National Park. The park is one of the largest protected areas in the country.

It is home to most of the larger mammals, including elephants, rhinos, buffalo, lions, leopards, and others. The park is a perfect destination for anyone who wants to enjoy a pristine wildlife experience. Other highlights of the park include the spectacular Mzima Springs and captivating landscapes.
