Career

In 1979, Baker-Finch became a professional. He attributes his greatest influence to Jack Nicklaus, claiming that he based his game on Nicklaus’ book, Golf My Way.

Baker-Finch started his professional career on the PGA Tour of Australasia, where he won his first tournament, the New Zealand Open, in 1983. In 1984, his victory earned him a spot in The Open Championship. He would create headlines by capturing the 36-hole lead, holding it for three rounds, but then finishing ninth after a terrible final round 79, identical to Bobby Clampett’s collapse two years prior.
