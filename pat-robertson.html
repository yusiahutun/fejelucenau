Pat Robertson Biography

Marion Gordon “Pat” Robertson was born on March 22, 1930, in Lexington, Virginia, to Gladys Churchill and Absalom Willis Robertson. Willis, his older brother, gave him the nickname “Pat.”

In 1946, he graduated from Chattanooga’s The McCallie School. He then earned a bachelor’s degree in history from Washington and Lee University. He was a member of the Sigma Alpha Epsilon fraternity there.

Career

Pat Robertson began his career in the military after being drafted. In 1948, he was reinstated. He was promoted to the first lieutenant in 1952. He graduated from Yale Law School in 1955 with a law degree. He received his Master of Divinity degree from The Biblical Seminary in New York in 1959. Robertson was ordained as a minister in the Southern Baptist Convention in 1961. He founded the Christian Broadcasting Network in Virginia a year later. He started by buying a station in Portsmouth. After purchasing the channel in 1977, he renamed it CBN.

CBN is currently available in 180 countries and has broadcast content in 71 languages. It was renamed CBN Family Channel in 1988 and then simply Family Channel. He founded International Family Entertainment Inc. two years later.

In 1977, he also established CBN University in Virginia Beach. It was renamed Regent University in 1989. He founded and served as president of the American Center for Law and Justice.
